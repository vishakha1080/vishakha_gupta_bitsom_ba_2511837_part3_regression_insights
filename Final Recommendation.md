\# Final Recommendation

\## Executive Summary

The objective of this analysis was to identify the factors most strongly associated with monthly sales performance across retail stores.

The final multiple regression model achieved an R² value of 0.8127, indicating that approximately 81.3% of the variation in monthly sales can be explained by the selected predictors.

This suggests that the model provides strong support for business decision-making.

\---

\## Key Drivers of Monthly Sales

The most influential factors associated with monthly sales are:

1\. Footfall

2\. Marketing Spend

3\. Inventory Availability Percentage

4\. Regional Performance (South and West Regions)

Among all variables, footfall emerged as the strongest predictor of sales performance.

\---

\## Variables Leadership Should Focus On

\### Increase Customer Footfall

Footfall has a strong positive and statistically significant relationship with sales.

Recommended actions:

\- Loyalty programs

\- Local marketing campaigns

\- Community engagement activities

\- Customer acquisition initiatives

\---

\### Continue Strategic Marketing Investment

Marketing spend remains statistically significant across the analysis.

Recommended actions:

\- Increase marketing efficiency

\- Track campaign ROI

\- Prioritize high-performing channels

\---

\### Improve Inventory Availability

Inventory availability shows a positive and statistically significant relationship with sales.

Recommended actions:

\- Reduce stockouts

\- Improve replenishment planning

\- Strengthen inventory forecasting

\---

\### Learn from South and West Regions

The South and West region dummy variables are statistically significant and positively associated with sales.

Recommended actions:

\- Investigate successful regional practices

\- Replicate high-performing operational strategies

\---

\## Variables That Should Not Be Over-Interpreted

\### Average Discount Percentage

Although included in the model, the variable is not statistically significant.

Leadership should avoid assuming that larger discounts automatically drive higher sales.

\### North Region

The North region coefficient is not statistically significant and should be interpreted cautiously.

\---

\## Risks and Limitations

The model has several limitations:

\- Regression identifies associations, not causation

\- Seasonal demand was not included

\- Competitive intensity was not included

\- Local economic factors were not included

\- Store-level management quality was not included

\---

\## Why Regression Does Not Prove Causation

Regression analysis identifies relationships between variables but cannot prove that one variable directly causes changes in another.

For example, marketing spend is associated with higher sales, but additional business experiments would be required to prove a direct causal impact.

Therefore, regression results should be used as decision-support evidence rather than proof of cause-and-effect relationships.

\---

\## Final Business Recommendation

Leadership should prioritize initiatives that increase customer footfall, maintain effective marketing investment, and improve inventory availability.

These factors demonstrate the strongest and most statistically reliable relationships with monthly sales performance and offer the greatest opportunity for sustainable business growth.
